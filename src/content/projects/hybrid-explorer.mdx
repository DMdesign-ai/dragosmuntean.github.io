## Overview

The Hybrid Explorer is an interactive table-like device designed for On retail stores. It allows customers to compare shoes side-by-side, explore product details, and discover new models through a physical-digital hybrid interface.

## Approach

The project required bridging the gap between physical retail and digital product information. Research focused on understanding in-store shopping behavior and identifying moments where digital augmentation could enhance the experience.

Key activities included:
- **In-Store Research** — Observed customer behavior and identified comparison pain points
- **Interaction Design** — Designed intuitive table-based interactions for product exploration
- **Prototyping** — Built interactive prototypes to test physical-digital interactions

## The Experience

Customers can place shoes on the table surface, which triggers product information displays, comparison views, and personalized recommendations. The interface is designed to feel intuitive and non-intrusive, enhancing rather than replacing the tactile shopping experience.

## Key Takeaway

The best digital experiences in physical spaces are the ones that feel invisible. The Hybrid Explorer succeeds because it augments natural shopping behavior rather than forcing customers into a new paradigm.
